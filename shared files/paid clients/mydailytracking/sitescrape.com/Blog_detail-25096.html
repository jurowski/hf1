No More New Year’s Resolutions!

Posted :  6/16/2010 5:45:07 PM  |

 0 Comments   

By  System Administrator

  

In my years as a business and life coach, I’ve learned one very important lesson; intentions are good, action is crucial. That’s why this year I am urging each and every one of you to do away with the New Years resolutions. Stop making promises to yourself saying “this year will be different”; or that you’ll stop doing one thing or start doing another. This can be a time of deceiving yourself or a time of taking action to achieve more.

Think about this, recent research shows that while 52% of participants in a resolution study were confident of success with their goals, only 12% actually achieved their goals. Men achieved their goal 22% more often when they engaged in goal setting, a system where small measurable goals are used (lose a pound a week, instead of saying “lose weight”), while women succeeded 10% more when they made their goals public and got support from their friends. (Quirkology Study)

So my advice to you… do away with the standard New Year’s Resolution and make a commitment today to improve yourself, your life, or your business in ONE WAY over the next 84 days.

If you think about it, anything is possible. On May 25, 1961, President John F. Kennedy announced the goal of sending astronauts to the moon before the end of the decade. Although it took quite a bit of time and resources to make a reality, the Apollo 11 astronauts were launched from Kennedy Space Centre. It took them ONLY 3 days, 3 hours, 49 minutes… to fly to the Moon.

What could you get done in 84 days?

Take a moment and think about what you would want to improve on and once you have it continue reading.

Write your goal down on a piece of paper (no typing for now)

Now, follow these simple steps to identify, clarify, visualize, and take the first steps toward making it a reality.

1.What will you name this goal? (Give it your own personalized name. For example, My
2.How long will it take to achieve it?
3.What step(s) need to happen to make it a reality?
4.What do you have to stop doing?
5.What will you need to start doing?
6.What could get in your way of succeeding?
7.Who do you need to support this goal?
8.Who (besides yourself) could potentially become an obstacle in achieving your goal?
9.When you achieve your goal, what will your life or business look like?
10.Assuming you’re already there, how will you feel when you accomplish this goal? (make sure to include detail)
Now that you have a plan, you’ll need accountability. If you’d like my support, I will be your advocate. If you need someone to kick you in the booty… I will. If you need some words of encouragement… I’m there.

This will be the main focus until the end of the year for our Facebook group. If you’re a member, I can’t wait to support you. If you’re not, join today. It’s free, fun and the only condition is we only accept positive people with a can do attitude. Let’s finish 2009 with a bang!

Imagine the possibilities!

Josh
