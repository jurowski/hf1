Why are the tough decisions in life so uncomfortable?

Posted :  6/16/2010 5:42:36 PM  |

 0 Comments   

By  System Administrator

  

If you’ve ever made a tough decision in life, than you know what I’m talking about when I say uncomfortable. What you’ll come to realize is it’s not necessarily the outcome or vision of the outcome that it so traumatic, but more so the actual decision that needs to be made. It’s a feeling of uncomfortable tension which comes from holding two conflicting thoughts in the mind at the same time; otherwise known as Cognitive dissonance.

Cognition = The process of thought

Dissonance = Lack of agreement, consistency, or harmony.

As you’ve probably noticed, this discomfort increases with relative importance, the level of conflicting thoughts, and/or the inability to rationalize the thought or action.

A good example of this would be if you believe that you are a honest person and you found a $100 bill on the ground in a coffee shop with no one else around. Most people believe that they would bring it up to the register and see if anyone lost it… but deep down, many people would consider taking the money. The point of discomfort with this decision would be considered Cognitive Dissonance and would probably haunt you for a little while; unless you were able to quickly rationalize keeping the money or giving it back.

So how do you stop feeling this discomfort you may ask?

Well, I’m sorry to say but you can’t completely stop it, however, you can limit it’s effects on you. To limit this type of discomfort in a situation you would need to either change or justify your behavior. So next time you begin feeling discomfort or on edge, take a moment and see if you can recognize the conflict. Once you’ve recognized the inner conflict, try and attend to how it came about and if it is something that is/was avoidable. If you can avoid this type of conflict next time, you may be able to now that you’ve recognized the pattern. It’s all about doing the right thing for you and what naturally feels right in your mind.
Like my mother always said “Always take the high road”. If you do, you’ll never need to look back and wonder why or if; and never have to question if you made the right decision.

I truly appreciate being a part of your journey. Go live the life you’ve always wanted to live TODAY!
