What do you believe?

Posted :  6/16/2010 5:08:26 PM  |

 0 Comments   

By  System Administrator

  

It’s funny to me how some days I need to find a good topic to discuss and some days the topic finds me; like this one did! I’ve been reminded of this so many times in the last few weeks within my life, in the media, and on TV, so I thought I should share.

 

It’s become “the norm” within groups of friends, standard at the workplace, and almost expected within the public/media arena. It’s a horrible habit that is so easy to fall in to… I’ve seen occur hundreds of times in my life…. people speaking poorly about other people behind their back. To be frank, on one hand it upsets me and on the other, I chuckle because all I can hear is my mom whispering the same thing in my ear time and time again “talk about people as if you were talking with their sister or brother”. Little did I understand the profound advice I was receiving as a child and I didn’t really get what she meant until many years later, but fortunately I’ve followed this advice my entire life (of course with a slip up here and there).

 

I guess what I’m getting at is if you are saying something that couldn’t be said to someone’s face, why say it at all? I mean really, what would be the benefit of talking trash about someone when they can’t even defend themselves? I know I know, sometimes it feel so good to vent, right? Think about this, what would happen if the person that was the recipient of the trash talking was able to hear you; to hear what you though of them? Do you think they would laugh it off? Or be able to walk away with their head held high? I’ll tell you one thing, I don’t know many people with such thick skin that they wouldn’t be hurt or upset by negative words or crude remarks by another person. Every time I watch someone get “busted” talking trash, it’s not the sender that is usually hurt. I can literally see the sadness in the eyes of the person who hears what you really think about them.

 

If you’re guilty of doing this sometimes, this isn’t to make you feel bad or for you to go apologize today. It’s just a simple life lesson reminder from my mom that I wanted to share with each of you.

 

Remember, “Talk about others as if you were talking to their sister or brother” and you will know that you’re taking the high road in life. There is nothing better than knowing that you did the right thing and followed your beliefs and values in a tough situation. Trust yourself to be the person you’ve always wanted to be.

 

You are my inspiration!

 

Thank you for allowing me to be part of your journey.

 

Josh

What do you believe?
October 27, 2009 by joshhirsch

In business and in life, I’ve seen a number of people succeed beyond their wildest dreams; others I’ve seen never getting what they truly want out of life. You can almost see them getting in their own way and hitting a metaphorical brick wall over and over and over again. Sometimes it’s tough to watch, but it’s a joy to watch the people who make the decision to make a breakthrough… and DO!

Why do I bring this up you may ask? It’s simple, there are people who decide to believe in themselves and people that don’t? There are others who will never think their good enough. Some people have been screwed over, dumped, or even abused. This is not saying it’s easy nor it is fair when someone else impedes on our life progress, but it does happen which means we can’t ignore it and we can’t allow others to determine our future; that decision my friend is up to you. The reality is… we’ve all created our own truths, our own perception in “our world”. The question is…. What story are we telling ourselves?

My story…

I believe that success is the outcome when desire outweighs fear…

I believe that strength is making the decision to believe in yourself; your thoughts and opinions

I believe that if you are not uncomfortable, you’re not growing

I believe that when you commit to doing something, you should follow through

I believe that independence is earned when you make the decision to create you own path and not follow others

I believe that one’s true self shows up when they feel that they’re up against a tough situation

I believe that courage is when someone stands by their beliefs and/or their opinion when society disagrees

I believe that where I am now, is a direct result of the decisions I’ve made up to this point

 

What do you believe?

 


Do you believe you can change the outcome of your life?

Do you believe that you can create certainty in your life?

Do you believe that you can achieve any goal you want to achieve?

Do you believe that you can change someone else’s life for the better… permanently?

I ask you this not to question who you are, but what you believe. I know that you are an amazing person with tremendous potential. My goal is to help you bring out the more productive, inspiring you; the one that only you’ve seen to this point. I don’t only want to change your day… I want to inspire your life. This is about believing in your abilities, your intelligence, your ideas, your talent and knowing that you have the key to your success. The key isn’t buried in a treasure chest somewhere that you need a map to get to; it’s inside you right now just waiting for you to unleash it.

So the question is… What do you believe? And what do you need to do to make sure that your beliefs support what you really want in life?

 

Remember, if you don’t believe in yourself, why should others believe in you?

 


Take the next 10-15 minutes to write down everything you believe about who you are and what your capabilities are in your business and life. If you can’t do it now, reread this later or email me and I’ll send you a copy of it to take with you. If you truly take the time to think about this, you will without a doubt have some interesting awareness’s.

My talent:

My intelligence:

My future:

My family relationships:

My spouse or significant other (or romantic relationships in general):

My communication:

My health:

My diet:

My career:
