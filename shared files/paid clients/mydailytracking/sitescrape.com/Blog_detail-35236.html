How to get past call reluctence

Posted :  6/16/2010 3:40:23 PM  |

 0 Comments   

By  System Administrator

  

One of my friends and people whom I coach asked me today how it’s possible that she can talk to anyone and talk for hours, but has call reluctance. Here is how I answered her… I thought it may help some of you. This is a huge issue, so more to come.
______________________________________________________________________

It’s a good question and many people have the exact same issue, so don’t feel bad. I currently work with a handful of people that are having the same issue and I thought I’d share with you what I tell them.

1. If your “why” is clear, than the calls become less of a strain and option and more of a means to an end. Meaning, if you were calling to raise money for a loved one who needed a new heart, you’d be able to pick up the phone without fail AND you would be proud and excited to dial, as opposed to fearful or dreading it.

2. A great deal of call reluctance from my own personal experience and from what I’ve seen over the years is not having a strong enough purpose for the call. If you’re able to articulate your message is a CLEAR, SUCCINCT manor, people will be tremendously appreciative that you took the time to connect with them. Think more along the lines of calling a friend to go for drinks vs. a client that you want to buy something or that you need to have do something (i.e. refer you).

3. Inevitably many times it just comes down to laziness and ineffective time management. Other things in the office are easier to complete, so why not, right? I typically begin with the hardest things first so that I’m only working my way down the list in responsibility vs moving toward the hardest to do last. It’s like with Crossfit, if you did the running, push ups, air squats first and then did the kettle bells and high jumps last (or your least favorite exercise last every time) Get it out of the way first if you can unless there is a method to your madness.

4. Set a small attainable goal for 2 weeks (5 calls per day), then move up toward more each week thereafter. So you may only add 1 or 2 calls each week, but over the course of a few months, you’ll get up to 20-30 calls which can keep most business afloat if not create success.

I will try and add to this later, but feel free to ask me more about any of these ideas…
